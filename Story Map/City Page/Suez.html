Egyptian Suez Canal The Suez Canal, opened in 1869, is a sea-level waterway in Egypt connecting the Mediterranean Sea to the Red Sea through the Isthmus of Suez.

The canal offers watercraft a more direct route between the North Atlantic and northern Indian oceans via the Mediterranean and Red seas, thus avoiding the South Atlantic and southern Indian oceans.

The Suez Canal, located at the junction of the three continents of Europe, Asia and Africa, is the longest ship lock-free canal in the world. It can be navigable day and night, and has a very high traffic and strategic position.
